## 1. The Universal Truths (Found in Every Tradition)

### 1.1 The Golden Rule (Variations in Every Culture)

| Tradition | Version |
|-------------|---------|
| Egyptian | "Do not do to others what you would not want done to you." (Ptahhotep, c. 2200 BCE) |
| Mesopotamian | "If a man says 'I will do to you what you did to me' — that is justice." (Code of Hammurabi, c. 1750 BCE) |
| Hindu | "This is the sum of duty: do not do to others what would cause pain to you." (Mahabharata, c. 200 BCE) |
| Buddhist | "Hurt not others in ways that you yourself would find hurtful." (Udana, c. 500 BCE) |
| Zoroastrian | "That nature alone is good which refrains from doing unto another whatsoever is not good for itself." (Dadistan-i-Dinik, c. 900 CE) |
| Chinese (Confucian) | "Do not do to others what you do not want done to yourself." (Analects, c. 500 BCE) |
| Norse | "Treat guests well, even if they are your enemies." (Hávamál, c. 1000 CE) |

**Modern insight:** The Golden Rule is **not** a religious invention — it's a **survival strategy**. Groups that practice reciprocity outcompete those that don't. Evolution selected for it 1,000,000 years before humans wrote it down.

**2026 application:** AI alignment. We want AI to follow the Golden Rule. Turns out, we've been training it on our own texts for 4,000 years.

## 2. The Differences (What Makes Each Unique)

While the **core truths** are universal, each tradition emphasizes a **unique path**:

| Tradition | Unique Contribution |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Egyptian | **Preservation** — knowledge must be saved for eternity (pyramids, texts) |
| Mesopotamian | **Law** — society needs written rules (Code of Hammurabi) |
| Hindu | **Psychology** — map of consciousness (Atman, Brahman, chakras) |
| Buddhist | **Meditation** — systematic training of the mind |
| Zoroastrian | **Ethics** — good vs. evil is a daily choice |
| Chinese (Taoist) | **Systems thinking** — stop forcing, trust emergence |
| Norse | **Resilience** — courage in the face of doom |

**2026 lesson:** We need **all** these paths. Preservation (digital archives), law (AI regulation), psychology (mental health), meditation (focus), ethics (alignment), systems thinking (policy), resilience (climate adaptation).
