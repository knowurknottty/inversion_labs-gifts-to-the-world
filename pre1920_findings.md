Patent search for aether/ether pre-1920 yields mostly:
- Chemical ether (anesthesia, solvents)
- Ether as fuel/gas
- "Ether" in wireless/radio transmission (metaphorical usage)
- Very few actual aether physics patents

## Key Insight: Aether was NOT patented

The luminiferous aether was a theoretical construct, not a technology. No patents exist for:
- Aether detection devices
- Aether manipulation apparatus
- Aether-based communication
- Aether engines/motors

Post-1920, "aether" patents appear in:
- Radio/wireless (metaphorical: "ether" as airwaves)
- Chemical processes (diethyl ether)
- New Age/alt-science devices (post-1960s)

## Most Important Pre-1920 Documents

1. **Whittaker (1910)** — "A history of the theories of aether and electricity" — THE canonical reference. Covers Descartes through 19th century.
   https://archive.org/details/historyoftheorie00whit

2. **Lodge (1909)** — "The Ether of Space" — Last major defense of aether before relativity. Experiments, philosophy, implications.
   https://archive.org/details/etherofspace00lodg

3. **Kelvin (1904)** — "Baltimore lectures on molecular dynamics and the wave theory of light" — Lord Kelvin's luminiferous aether lectures.
   https://archive.org/details/baltimorelecture00kelv

4. **Michelson & Morley (1887)** — "On the Relative Motion of the Earth and the Luminiferous Ether" — The experiment that (supposedly) killed aether. Published in American Journal of Science, not a book.
   https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_Relative_Motion_of_the_Earth_and_the_Luminiferous_Ether

5. **Maxwell (1865/1873)** — "A Dynamical Theory of the Electromagnetic Field" / "Treatise on Electricity and Magnetism" — Maxwell's equations originally formulated WITH aether. The aether was implicit, not explicit.
   https://archive.org/details/electricandmagne01maxw

6. **Lorentz (1895/1909)** — "Versuch einer Theorie der electrischen und optischen Erscheinungen in bewegten Körpern" — Lorentz's electron theory retained aether as the stationary medium. English translation: "The Theory of Electrons" (1909).

7. **FitzGerald (1889)** — "The Ether and the Earth's Atmosphere" — Proposed length contraction to explain Michelson-Morley null result. Short paper, huge impact.

8. **Larmor (1900)** — "Aether and Matter" — Mathematical treatment of aether dynamics.
   https://archive.org/details/aetherandmatter00larm
